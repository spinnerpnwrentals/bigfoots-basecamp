# Brand Voice

Bigfoot's Basecamp should sound like a knowledgeable local friend welcoming guests to a favorite mountain cabin.

Not a luxury hotel.

Not a tourism bureau.

Not a corporate vacation rental.

The tone should feel genuine, relaxed, conversational, and occasionally humorous.

---

# Personality

- Friendly
- Welcoming
- Helpful
- Relaxed
- Confident
- Authentic
- Mountain-loving

Never stuffy.

Never corporate.

Never overly polished.

---

# Humor

Humor should appear naturally.

Think:

- "Calories don't count after hiking."
- "If you're skiing first chair, coffee should probably be your first stop."
- "One favor before heading inside: pull the hot tub cover back over so it's nice and warm for the next soak."

Use occasional mountain jokes.

Occasional Bigfoot jokes.

Never overdo them.

---

# Writing Style

Use contractions.

Write the way real people talk.

Short paragraphs.

Easy to skim.

Avoid buzzwords like:

- Luxury
- Premium
- Experience
- Adventure
- World-class

Instead, describe places honestly.

---

# Bigfoot

Bigfoot is the brand.

Not every page needs to talk about Bigfoot.

The Bigfoot theme should mostly live in:

- Scavenger Hunt
- Branding
- Small easter eggs
- Occasional jokes

The rest of the site should focus on helping guests have a great stay.

---

# Recommendations

When recommending restaurants or hikes:

Explain why we'd actually go there.

Not just what it is.

Example:

"We stop here almost every trip because the coffee is consistently excellent and it's a perfect way to start a mountain morning."

---

# Basecamp Tips

Every page should include one or two "Basecamp Tips."

These should sound like advice from the owners.

---

# Overall Goal

Guests should feel like they're getting recommendations from friends, not reading a manual.
