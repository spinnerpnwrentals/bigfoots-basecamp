Family Activities

Easy ideas for keeping the whole crew happy.

Whether you're here for a ski weekend, summer hiking, or a slower mountain getaway, Snoqualmie Pass has plenty for every age. These are some of our favorite family-friendly options near Basecamp.

Perfect for Little Kids

Easy Wins for All Ages

Easy Family Favorite Stroller Friendly

Gold Creek Pond

A beautiful paved trail looping around an alpine pond with big mountain views. Perfect for young kids, grandparents, and strollers.

Why We Love It

Probably the easiest way to get big mountain scenery with very little walking.

Scenic Waterfall All Ages

Snoqualmie Falls

One of Washington's most iconic waterfalls, with easy viewing platforms and a short walk.

Why We Love It

Kids love the huge waterfall, and adults don't exactly suffer through the views either.

Winter Family Favorite

Summit Tubing Center

A fun winter activity for kids and adults alike. Reserve tickets early during holidays.

Why We Love It

A great option for families that don't ski, or for skiers who want a low-effort snow day.

Outdoor Adventures

Trails, Creeks, and Big Mountain Moments

Waterfall Family Hike

Franklin Falls

A relatively short hike to one of the area's prettiest waterfalls. Trail conditions vary a lot in winter, so check before you go.

Summer Creek Family

Denny Creek

Kids love exploring the creek, forest, and natural rock slides during warmer months.

Lake Hiking Adventure

Snow Lake

One of Washington's classic alpine hikes for families with older kids who are ready for a challenge.

Rainy Day Fun

North Bend

Explore local coffee shops, bookstores, and a few fun small-town shops.

Twede's Cafe

Grab breakfast or pie while visiting the famous Twin Peaks diner.

North Bend Theatre

A classic small-town movie theater for a low-key evening.

Board Games at Basecamp

Enjoy a game night around the dining table after a day outside. Snacks strongly encouraged.

Our Family's Perfect Day

Morning

Coffee for the adults, then a Gold Creek Pond walk.

Lunch

Basecamp Adventure Tips

A Little Preparation Goes a Long Way

Start Early

Start hikes early during summer weekends.

Layer Up

Dress in layers and be ready for changing mountain weather.

Bring Water

Always bring water, even for short outings.

Watch the Weather

Weather changes quickly around Snoqualmie Pass.

Observe Wildlife Safely

Kids love spotting wildlife; please observe from a safe distance.

Leave No Trace

Leave No Trace so future families can enjoy these places too.
